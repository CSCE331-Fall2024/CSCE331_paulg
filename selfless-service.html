My Selfless Service

Big Event

During the Big Event of my freshman year, I had the opportunity to participate in a community service project at a local church. Our task was to clean the entire building and create Easter eggs for children. The cleanup took over two hours, requiring us to work efficiently and collaboratively to cover every area. This experience taught me the importance of teamwork, as we had to coordinate our efforts and communicate effectively to complete the task within the given time. Working alongside my peers, I learned how to delegate responsibilities and support one another, ensuring that we accomplished our goal. The process of creating Easter eggs for the children was particularly rewarding, knowing that our efforts would bring joy to others. Overall, I found the experience to be both enjoyable and educational, as it reinforced the value of selfless service and the impact we can have when we work together.

Daughters of the American Revolution Headstone Cleaning

I had the opportunity to volunteer with the George Washington Chapter of the Daughters of the American Revolution, where our task was to clean and restore headstones in a local cemetery. Over the course of four hours, I worked alongside other volunteers to meticulously clean 14 large headstones, ensuring that each one was treated with care and respect. The experience was both physically demanding and deeply rewarding, as we aimed to preserve the memory of those who had come before us. It was important to make the area look presentable, not only out of respect for the deceased but also for the families and visitors who come to pay their respects. This experience taught me the value of dedication and teamwork, as we had to coordinate our efforts to achieve our goal. Overall, I found the work meaningful, and it was fulfilling to contribute to such an important cause.
